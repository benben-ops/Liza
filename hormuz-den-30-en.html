Analogy 1 — OPEC Oil Embargo 1973–74
🛢 OPEC Oil Embargo 1973–74 (Yom Kippur War)
Trigger
Yom Kippur War. Egypt+Syria attack Israel. US supports Israel → OAPEC imposes embargo on the US, Europe, and Japan.
Shock mechanism
OPEC production cuts: −4.5 mb/d (~7% of supply). Oil price +300% over 6 months. The West primarily affected.
Outcome / duration
5 months. Stagflation for a decade. US GDP −3%, unemployment 9%, inflation 12.3%.
Policy response
IEA founded 1974. US establishes SPR. France accelerates its nuclear programme.
Key difference vs. 2026
1973: −4.5 mb/d. 2026: Hormuz blocks 20 mb/d — a 4.4× larger shock. 1973: the West affected. 2026: most vulnerable is the Asian manufacturing belt (80% of Hormuz oil flows to Asia). 1973: war ended before the embargo. 2026: blockade runs concurrently with active hostilities.
Analogy 2 — Tanker War & Operation Earnest Will 1987–88
⚓ Iran–Iraq Tanker War — Hormuz 1980–88
Trigger
Iran–Iraq War 1980–88. Both sides attack tankers. US launches Op. Earnest Will — escorting Kuwaiti tankers.
Shock mechanism
Selective attacks on tankers (not a total blockade). The strait remains partially functional.
Outcome / duration
8 years of partial disruptions. Op. Praying Mantis 1988: US destroys Iranian warships.
Policy response
International escort operations. Iranian naval capacity dramatically weakened.
Key difference vs. 2026
1987: selective attacks, strait partially functional. 2026: de facto total halt (5 ships/day vs. 138 normal). Asymmetric drones and mines are now cheap and mass-produced — escort is insufficient.

✅ Solution 1 — "Hormuz Protocol": International Strait Management
Mechanism
Multilateral agreement US–Iran–China–India–EU–UN: formalises "free but coordinated" transit. Model: Panama Canal. IMO as manager of transit rules with Iranian oversight but no monopoly.
Why it works
Addresses Iran's demand for "sovereignty recognition" diplomatically. China has an incentive to pressure Iran (45% of its oil via Hormuz). India can mediate. UN sponsorship provides legitimacy.
Implementation
Phase 1 (1–2 weeks): temporary ceasefire. Phase 2 (2–6 months): permanent protocol. Phase 3: international management active. Requires halt to strikes on civilian infrastructure as a goodwill gesture.
✅ Solution 2 — Asian Energy Security Treaty
Mechanism
Japan, Korea, India, ASEAN, Australia formalise a joint pact: shared strategic reserves, joint LNG procurement, diversification away from the Middle East. Model: IEA for Asia.
Why it works
Reduces strategic vulnerability to any future Hormuz shock. Japan, Korea: acute crisis motivation. China can be invited — it has a stake in supply stability.
Implementation
Emergency summit Tokyo/Seoul/New Delhi within 3 weeks. First round: 200 M bbl joint reserves. Target: reduce Hormuz dependence by 50% by 2035.
✅ Solution 3 — Humanitarian Corridor as Diplomatic Bridge
Mechanism
Expand Iran's 27 Mar. consent for humanitarian shipments into a full corridor under UN auspices: food, medicine, fertilisers, civilian fuel. Iran gains positive PR. WFP and FAO as guarantors.
